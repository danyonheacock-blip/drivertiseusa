Mobile Advertising — Design Guide
Design for Impact

Your ad has
2 seconds.

Mobile ads are seen at 35 mph, across a parking lot, or in a glance at a stoplight. This guide is everything you need to make those 2 seconds count — so your campaign performs the way it should.

The single most important thing to understand: Mobile advertising is not the same as a flyer, a Facebook ad, or a business card. It's a moving billboard. Design for a glance — not a reader. If someone has to slow down or squint to read it, it doesn't work.
Rule 01

One Message. One Glance.

Pick the single thing you want someone to walk away knowing. Your business name and what you do. Or a phone number. Not both, not five things — one. Every element you add competes for the same 2 seconds of attention. Only the winner gets remembered.

Strong: "Smith Plumbing · (801) 555-0100"
Weak: Logo + tagline + 3 services + address + website + QR code
Rule 02

Go Big or Go Unread.

If you have to squint at a proof to read it, it won't work on the road. Your business name should be large enough to read from 30 feet away. When you're looking at your design on screen at full zoom, ask yourself: could I read this at half that size, instantly?

Minimum on final print: Business name ≥ 3" tall · Phone or URL ≥ 1.5" tall
Rule 03

High Contrast Only.

Your eyes find contrast, not color. Light text on dark background, or dark text on light background — that's what works. Avoid yellow on white, dark blue on black, or any combination where the text blends into the background even slightly. Test it: squint at your design from arm's length.

Works: White text on black · Black text on white · Gold on dark navy
Doesn't: Red on dark green · Light gray on white
Rule 04

Phone or Website. Not Both.

You get one call to action. For local service businesses (HVAC, landscaping, plumbing, cleaning), use the phone number — people call. For retail, restaurants, or e-commerce, use the website. Splitting attention between two actions means neither one sticks.

Service business: Phone number, large
Retail/online: Website URL, short and clean
Rule 05

Name + Logo — Not Just a Logo.

Most people don't know your logo yet. They need your name spelled out in readable text. Your logo supports the name — it doesn't replace it. "Alpine HVAC" in bold type is more effective than a stylized "AH" mark that nobody recognizes yet.

Right: Full business name in large readable text, logo as accent
Wrong: Logo only, name in tiny subtext
Rule 06

Breathing Room Matters.

White space (or empty background space) is not wasted space — it gives the eye somewhere to land and makes important elements pop. A cluttered ad is a confusing ad. Leave generous breathing room around your business name and contact info. The simpler it looks, the more professional it feels.

Aim for: 3–4 elements max — name, what you do, contact, logo. That's it.

Quick Reference — Do's and Don'ts

✓ Do

  • Keep it to 5–7 words of body text maximum
  • Use your actual brand colors (high contrast pairings only)
  • Make the business name the dominant visual element
  • Use a clean background — solid or very simple gradient
  • Round corners on design elements for a modern, professional feel
  • Have someone else read it from 10 feet away and describe it back

✗ Don't

  • Use more than two fonts
  • Include your full address (nobody reads it at speed)
  • Use busy photo backgrounds behind text
  • Use thin or script fonts for anything important
  • Include more than one call to action
  • Design for print and assume it works as a vehicle ad

Artwork File Requirements

Requirement Specification
File Format PDF (preferred) or PNG — no JPG, no Word documents, no Canva screenshots
Resolution 300 DPI minimum at final print size — lower resolution will look blurry on print
Color Mode CMYK for most accurate print color — RGB may shift colors slightly at print
Dimensions See your Magnetic Ad Spec Sheet for exact door panel dimensions and bleed requirements
Fonts Embed all fonts in the file or convert text to outlines before exporting
Bleed Add 0.125" bleed on all sides — keeps the design clean at the cut edge
