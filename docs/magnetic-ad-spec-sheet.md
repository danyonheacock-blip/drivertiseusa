## Design Guidelines

**Keep it simple.** Your sign will be seen at 30–45 mph. Viewers have 2–3 seconds.

The formula that works:
1. **Business name** — large, readable font
2. **What you do** — one line or one word
3. **How to reach you** — phone number or website, large enough to read from a car

**What to avoid:**
- More than 3 lines of text
- Small fonts (nothing under 48pt at final size)
- Busy backgrounds that compete with your text
- QR codes as the primary CTA (hard to scan on a moving vehicle)

**Colors:** High contrast wins. Dark background + light text, or light background + dark text. Avoid yellow text on white or dark text on dark backgrounds.

## Placement Notes (2014 Volvo S60)

Your sign will be placed on the flat panel area of the door. A few things we work around on this vehicle:

- **Character line:** The S60 has a horizontal body crease running across the doors. Your sign sits in the flat zone below this line.
- **Door handle:** Centered vertically on the door. Your sign clears it — no overlap.
- **Rear wheel arch:** The rear door curves toward the wheel well at the bottom. Rear signs are centered to avoid this.
- **Edges:** Signs are kept at least 1" from all door edges to maintain adhesion.

Nothing you need to design around — just so you know what we're working with.
